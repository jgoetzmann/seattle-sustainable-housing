"I believe that every American should have stable, dignified housing; ... the most very basic needs to sustain modern life, should be guarenteed in a moral society" - AOC


The population of the Seattle metropolitan area stands at 4.02 million, ranking it as the 15th-largest metropolitan area in the United States. Over the decade from 2010 to 2020, the region experienced a substantial growth rate of 21.1%. This rapid expansion has had significant implications for the housing market, with the median home price in Seattle nearly doubling from $409,000 in 2010 to $749,000 in 2020.

Unfortunately, the surge in homes has resulted in financial strain, causing thousands of individuals in Seattle to face foreclosure on their homes. This situation has deprived them of their residences and profoundly impacted their livelihoods for the worse.

The plight of those forced out of their homes underscores the urgent need for support within the community. Addressing the housing crisis and assisting those affected can be crucial in helping them rebuild their lives and regain stability. Initiatives such as affordable housing programs, financial counseling, and community support networks could play a vital role in aiding individuals and families affected by the challenges posed by this unprecedented rapid growth.
